    • Wesley Lobato Passos was born in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, in 1991. He received his D.Sc. in Electrical Engineering from COPPE/UFRJ, Rio de Janeiro, RJ, Brazil, in 2024. Previously, he received his M.Sc. in Electrical Engineering also from COPPE/UFRJ in 2019, and his B.Sc. in Control Systems and Automation Engineering from the Federal Center for Technological Education (CEFET-RJ), Nova Iguaçu, RJ, Brazil, in 2016.

    During his undergraduate studies, he studied abroad at the University of Georgia (UGA), USA, as part of the Brazilian exchange program Science without Borders. During this period, he also conducted a summer research project at the Illinois Institute of Technology (IIT), in 2015.

    He received the Google Latin American Research Award in 2019 and 2020 for the project automatic detection of potential Aedes aegypti mosquito breeding grounds using computer vision and machine learning techniques.

    Since 2024, he is a Professor with the Control Systems and Automation Engineering Department, at the CEFET-RJ. He is a member of the Institute of Electric and Electronic Engineers (IEEE) and the Brazilian Telecommunication Society (SBrT).

    He has a background in Instrumentation, Control, and Industrial Automation projects. His research interests include Computer Vision, Machine Learning, and Signal Processing.
